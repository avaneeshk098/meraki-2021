Inked Solace

The word ‘solace’ is derived from a latin word, ‘solacium’ which means “to console”. Poetry allows for expression of thought, knowledge, inner-feelings and wisdom. Many write poetry as a means to find comfort and to cope with what they are going through. Through expression, we grow.

“Poetry is an echo, asking a shadow to dance.” -Carl Sandbury

Many of us often tend to turn to books in times of distress, seeking comfort and warmth in the words. Sometimes, we call up our friends and talk to them about our grievances, lightening our burden through sharing. Let this poetry event organized under ‘Meraki Literature Day’ give you a creative and free space to let your words touch our hearts! With a ticking clock and a theme for you to interpret and write, we give you Inked Solace. Let your words, flawless, catch our sights!

Guidelines

  • All poems must have a title and must not exceed 40 lines in length (excluding title). Must be read within the time limit mentioned.
  • Poems must be the entrant’s original work.
  • Entries must not have been published, self-published, published on a website or made public on social media, broadcast or featured
  • Do not use “texting” lingo. Properly spell out all words and numbers.
  • Poems containing language that is vulgar, offensive, or wholly inappropriate will not be accepted.
  • Please use trigger warnings and/or content where necessary to present the poem.
  • Contest entries are judged based on poetic technique, effectiveness, style, and creativity, content, originality.
  • Prepare a small description about the intent behind the poem before presenting it ( not more than 2 lines).
  • Poem presentations will follow a particular order and we would appreciate it if participants could stick to the order and present their submissions when called.
  • Each participant will be given 25 minutes to write a poem/ song based on the given theme
  • Each participant will also be given x minutes to present the poem

Rubrics

  • Creativity - 5 points
  • Originality - 5 points
  • Confidence and delivery - 5 points
  • Fluency - 5 points
  • Adherence to theme - 5 points

Theme

Espejo en la pared (mirror, mirror on the wall)

Description: Espejo en la pared is spanish for “mirror, mirror on the wall”...urging all you young poets/singers to use this opportunity to showcase your talents and interpret these words to the best of your imagination. We hope to see a reflection of your creativity from the words you write.
